Abstract 
 
The welfare of domestic cats and kittens has become an increasingly important concern as pet 
ownership rises and adoption rates increase. Many new owners lack access to reliable, centralised, 
and evidence -based guidance on responsible cat care, veterinary services, and ethical adoption 
practices. This contextual report explores the development of PurrfectStart, a comprehensive web-
based platform designed to address these challenges by integrating educational resources, adoption 
support, veterinary service discovery, and community interaction into a single digital environment.  
 
The report critically examines existing research on animal welfare, digital information platforms, 
and online communities, identifying gaps in current solutions that often fragment information 
across multiple sources. A hybrid Agile-Waterfall development methodology is justified to balance 
academic structure with iterative improvement. Product research, requirements analysis, and 
ethical, legal, and professional considerations are exp lored in depth to contextualise the proposed 
system within current technological and societal practices. The findings support the need for an 
accessible, user-centred platform that promotes responsible ownership, reduces abandonment, and 
contributes positi vely to animal welfare. This report provides the theoretical and practical 
foundation for the development of the PurrfectStart platform.

2. Introduction 
This section contextualises the project by revisiting the original proposal and refining its focus 
based on further research, reflection, and critical evaluation. While the initial proposal established 
the motivation for developing a web-based platform for cat and kitten care, the intervening period 
of research has enabled a deeper understanding of the problem space, existing solutions, and 
realistic project constraints. As a result, the scope, objectives, and development approach have been 
refined to ensur e academic rigour, technical feasibility, and meaningful contribution to animal 
welfare and digital information systems. 
The following subsections outline the refined background context, revised aims and objectives, the 
practical approach adopted, and a justification of the chosen methodological framework. 
 
2.1 Background 
The original project proposal identified a growing concern surrounding domestic cat welfare, 
particularly among first -time pet owners who lack access to reliable, structured, and centralised 
guidance. Subsequent research has reinforced this concern, reveal ing that many existing digital 
resources for cat care are fragmented, inconsistent in quality, or overly commercialised. As a result, 
users are often required to consult multiple platforms to obtain information on care practices, 
veterinary services, adoption processes, and peer support. 
Further investigation into animal welfare literature highlights that inadequate owner education is a 
recurring contributor to preventable health issues, behavioural problems, and, in severe cases, 
abandonment. This problem is exacerbated by the increasing reliance on informal online sources, 
such as social media groups and unmoderated forums, where misinformation can spread easily. 
While these platforms provide emotional support and anecdotal advice, they often lack 
accountability, evidence-based content, and structured moderation. 
From a technological perspective, modern web platforms provide opportunities to consolidate high-
quality information, services, and community interaction within a single environment. However, 
many existing pet care platforms focus narrowly on one function,  such as adoption listings or 
informational blogs, without addressing the broader ecosystem of responsible ownership. This 
background research has clarified the need for an integrated, user -centred platform that balances 
educational authority, accessibility, and community engagement. 
Consequently, the project’s focus has evolved from simply “providing information” to addressing 
how information is structured, validated, and socially reinforced within a digital environment that 
supports long-term responsible behaviour.

The approach involves cycles of: 
● Research and reflection to inform design decisions,

This adaptive process acknowledges that understanding of the problem space evolves alongside

By recognising the iterative and sometimes uncertain nature of the development process, the project

3. Literature Review 
The purpose of this literature review is to situate the PurrfectStart project within existing academic 
and professional research related to animal welfare, digital information platforms, online 
communities, and user -centred system design. Rather than catal oguing individual studies, this 
review synthesises key themes and debates to construct a coherent argument that justifies the need 
for the proposed platform and informs its design. In doing so, it follows the guidance that a literature 
review should function as a critical narrative leading into the proposed study rather than a sequential 
summary of sources. 
 
3.1 Approach to Literature Searching 
The literature search process was guided by the need to understand both the social problem of 
domestic cat welfare and the digital mechanisms through which information, services, and 
communities are delivered. Sources were identified using a combination of  academic databases, 
professional publications, and authoritative organisational resources. 
Academic literature was primarily sourced from databases such as Google Scholar and institutional 
library collections, focusing on peer -reviewed journals and academic books related to animal 
welfare, human –computer interaction, online communities, and info rmation systems design. 
Search terms included combinations of phrases such as responsible pet ownership, animal welfare 
education, online communities, information credibility, user-centred design, and digital health or 
welfare platforms. These terms were r efined iteratively as patterns and gaps emerged during the 
review process. 
In addition to academic sources, professional and organisational publications were consulted to 
ground the study in real -world practice. Resources from recognised animal welfare organisations 
and usability experts were included to complement academic theor y with applied guidance. This 
mixed approach ensured that the review reflected both conceptual perspectives and practical 
realities. 
12 
Bibliographic management software was used to organise sources, track citations, and generate 
references in a consistent format aligned with Harvard -style requirements. This supported 
systematic comparison of sources and reduced the risk of unstructured or anecdotal referencing. 
 
3.2 Identifying the Problem 
The literature consistently identifies education as a central factor influencing responsible pet 
ownership and animal welfare outcomes. Research in animal behaviour and welfare suggests that 
inadequate owner knowledge is closely linked to preventable health issues, behavioural difficulties, 
and abandonment. This problem is particularly pronounced among first -time owners, who may 
underestimate the long-term responsibilities associated with pet care. 
At the same time, studies of online information behaviour reveal that individuals increasingly rely 
on digital platforms as their primary source of guidance. While this creates opportunities for 
widespread dissemination of knowledge, it also introduces cha llenges related to information 
quality, credibility, and consistency. Several authors highlight that online pet care information is 
often fragmented across blogs, forums, commercial sites, and social media, making it difficult for 
users to assess reliability or completeness. 
The literature also points to a tension between professional authority and peer -generated content. 
While veterinary and welfare organisations provide evidence-based guidance, users frequently turn 
to peer communities for experiential advice and emotional s upport. Without appropriate 
moderation or contextualisation, these environments can inadvertently normalise misinformation 
or unsafe practices. 
Taken together, these findings suggest that the core problem is not merely a lack of information, 
but a lack of structured, trustworthy, and socially reinforced knowledge  within accessible 
digital environments. This problem framing moves beyond surface -level content provision and 
highlights the need for integrated systems that combine authoritative guidance with community 
interaction. 
 
3.3 Education, Trust, and Community in Digital Welfare 
Platforms 
A recurring theme in the literature is the relationship between education, trust, and community 
engagement in digital platforms. Research on online learning and information systems indicates 
that users are more likely to engage with and retain information when it is presented in a clear, 
structured manner and reinforced through social interaction. However, trust in digital content is 
13 
influenced not only by source credibility but also by interface design, transparency, and governance 
mechanisms. 
Studies of online communities demonstrate that moderated environments with clear norms are more 
effective at fostering constructive dialogue and knowledge exchange. Conversely, unmoderated or 
poorly governed forums tend to prioritise anecdotal experience o ver evidence -based guidance, 
which can undermine welfare outcomes. This is particularly problematic in domains involving 
health or care, where inaccurate advice may have serious consequences. 
Importantly, the literature suggests that effective platforms do not position professional knowledge 
and community knowledge as competing forces. Instead, successful systems integrate these forms 
of knowledge by providing authoritative reference points whi le allowing users to contextualise 
information through shared experience. This synthesis supports learning, confidence-building, and 
responsible decision-making. 
This body of research underpins the conceptual foundation of the proposed study. It supports the 
argument that a platform such as PurrfectStart should not function solely as an informational 
repository, nor as an unstructured social forum, but as a careful ly designed hybrid that aligns 
education, trust, and community interaction. 
 
3.4 Implications for User-Centred and Ethical Design 
The literature further emphasises the role of user -centred design in ensuring that digital welfare 
platforms are accessible, inclusive, and effective. Research in human –computer interaction 
highlights that poor usability, excessive cognitive load, and inaccessible interfaces can undermine 
even high -quality content. Accessibility standards and usability principles are therefore not 
peripheral concerns but integral to ethical and effective system design. 
Ethical considerations also feature prominently in the literature, particularly regarding 
responsibility for content accuracy and user influence. Authors caution that platforms addressing 
welfare-related issues must clearly distinguish between informationa l guidance and professional 
advice, and must implement safeguards against harm caused by misinformation or social pressure. 
These insights reinforce the importance of design decisions that prioritise clarity, transparency, and 
user protection. They also support the need for explicit moderation policies, disclaimers, and 
content governance structures within the proposed platform. 
 
3.5 Conclusions

This literature review has identified a clear convergence of issues related to animal welfare 
education, digital information credibility, and community-based knowledge sharing. The reviewed 
literature demonstrates that fragmented information systems, unmod erated online communities, 
and poor usability collectively contribute to suboptimal welfare outcomes for domestic animals. 
The key insight taken forward is that effective digital interventions must integrate authoritative 
knowledge, ethical governance, and user -centred design within a single coherent system. This 
understanding directly informs the proposed study by justifying the development of an integrated 
web-based platform rather than isolated features or content repositories. 
The findings of this review influence subsequent stages of the project by shaping the system 
requirements, feature selection, and design priorities. In particular, they emphasise the need for 
structured educational content, moderated community interaction,  and accessibility -conscious 
design. These principles form the conceptual bridge between the literature and the practical 
development of the PurrfectStart platform. 
 
4. Product Research 
The purpose of the product research phase is to evaluate existing digital platforms operating within 
the pet care, adoption, and veterinary information domain in order to contextualise the design and 
usability decisions for the proposed system. Rather than assessing technical implementation details, 
this analysis focuses on how effectively similar platforms support user goals through their interface 
design, content structure, and interaction patterns. The findings of this evaluation inform usability 
priorities and highlight design trade-offs relevant to the development of the PurrfectStart platform. 
 
4.1 Comparing Similar Products or Platforms Against a Set 
of Usability Criteria 
A comparative usability analysis was conducted on a selected set of existing pet care and animal 
welfare platforms. The platforms were chosen based on their relevance to the project’s core 
functions, namely educational content provision, adoption facilitat ion, veterinary service 
discovery, and community interaction. The intention was not to identify a single “best” product, 
but to examine how different design approaches succeed or fail in supporting user needs. 
To ensure consistency, platforms were evaluated against a common set of usability criteria derived 
from established usability principles and human –computer interaction literature. These criteria 
included: 
15 
● Learnability, referring to how easily first-time users can understand the platform structure 
and navigate key features.  
 
● Efficiency, relating to how quickly users can complete common tasks once familiar with 
the system.  
 
● Information clarity, assessing the organisation, readability, and coherence of content.  
 
● Accessibility, considering responsiveness, visual contrast, and accommodation of diverse 
user needs.  
 
● User trust and credibility cues, such as transparency of information sources, professional 
endorsements, and moderation visibility.  
 
The analysis revealed that platforms focusing primarily on informational content often perform 
well in terms of information depth but struggle with engagement and navigation efficiency. Dense 
text layouts and weak information hierarchies can overwhelm user s, particularly those seeking 
quick guidance. Conversely, adoption-focused platforms tend to prioritise visual appeal and search 
functionality, yet frequently provide limited contextual education, assuming a level of prior 
knowledge that may not exist among first-time adopters. 
Veterinary directory platforms typically offer efficient search and filtering tools but often lack 
meaningful explanations of how services differ or how users should interpret reviews. This places 
a cognitive burden on users to make decisions without sufficient guidance, potentially undermining 
trust. Community-based platforms, while effective at fostering peer interaction, frequently exhibit 
usability weaknesses related to content discoverability and moderation clarity. In such 
environments, valuable advice may be buried within long discussion threads, reducing its practical 
usefulness. 
Across platforms, accessibility emerged as an inconsistent consideration. While many systems 
demonstrate mobile responsiveness, fewer exhibit deliberate attention to readability, clear 
navigation cues, or reduced cognitive load. Importantly, platforms that scored well across multiple 
usability criteria tended to balance structured content with intuitive navigation rather than relying 
solely on visual design or feature quantity. 
This comparative analysis highlights a recurring usability gap: few platforms successfully integrate 
education, service discovery, and community interaction within a cohesive and user -centred 
interface. These findings directly inform the design rationale f or PurrfectStart, which seeks to 
prioritise clarity, guided navigation, and balanced information presentation. By learning from the 
usability strengths and limitations of existing platforms, the proposed system aims to reduce user 
effort, support informed decision-making, and improve overall user experience.

 5. Legal, Social, Ethical and Professional Issues and 
Considerations 
The development of a digital platform concerned with animal welfare, community interaction, and 
user-generated content raises a set of interrelated legal, social, ethical, and professional 
considerations. These considerations cannot be meaningfully separat ed, as legal compliance 
influences ethical responsibility, social impact shapes professional obligations, and design 
decisions mediate all three. This section therefore addresses these aspects holistically, focusing on 
how they shape both the scope and implementation of the proposed system. 
From a legal perspective, the most directly applicable legislation relates to data protection and 
privacy. The platform requires user registration to enable participation in community forums, 
submission of adoption listings, and interaction with veterinary  service reviews. As such, the 
collection and processing of personal data must comply with the UK General Data Protection 
Regulation and the Data Protection Act 2018. This includes lawful data processing, transparency 
regarding data usage, secure storage, and the provision of user rights such as access, rectification, 
and deletion. Other legislation, such as consumer protection law and e-commerce regulations, was 
considered but deemed less applicable, as the platform does not directly facilitate commercial 
transactions or provide paid services. 
Social considerations are closely linked to the ethical responsibilities of providing information 
related to animal welfare. The literature indicates that poorly governed online spaces can normalise 
misinformation and harmful practices. Consequently, the p latform must balance openness and 
inclusivity with safeguards that protect both animals and users. Community moderation policies, 
clear content guidelines, and the visible distinction between informational guidance and 
professional veterinary advice are therefore essential. These measures serve both a social function, 
by fostering a supportive and respectful community, and an ethical function, by reducing the risk 
of harm caused by inaccurate or misleading advice. 
Ethically, the project assumes a duty of care toward animals, users, and associated professionals. 
Care guidance must be evidence-informed and regularly reviewed, while disclaimers must clearly 
communicate the limits of the platform’s responsibility. Adopt ion-related features must avoid 
encouraging impulsive behaviour and instead promote informed, responsible decision -making. 
This ethical stance directly influences design decisions, such as the structuring of adoption listings, 
the presentation of care information, and the moderation of peer discussions. 
Professional considerations further reinforce these responsibilities. As a computing project, the 
system is expected to align with recognised professional standards, including those related to public 
interest, competence, and accountability. This includes maintaining documentation clarity, 
designing for accessibility, and ensuring that technical decisions can be justified academically and 
ethically. Collectively, these legal, social, ethical, and professional considerations form a constraint 
framework within which system requirements are defined and evaluated.

6. Requirements 
The requirements for the PurrfectStart platform have been derived through a combination of 
literature review, product research, and reflective analysis of the project’s legal, ethical, and 
professional context. Rather than treating requirements as purely technical specifications, they are 
framed as responses to identified user needs, usability challenges, and governance responsibilities. 
 
6.1 Analysis of Requirements 
In this project, requirements arise from the need to support responsible cat ownership while 
mitigating risks associated with misinformation, poor usability, and inadequate governance. The 
analysis indicates that users require more than access to informati on; they require structured 
guidance, contextual support, and intuitive interaction mechanisms. 
Key requirement drivers include: 
● The need for credible, structured educational content accessible to non -expert users.  
 
● The requirement for ethical facilitation of adoption without assuming liability for 
outcomes. 
 
● The necessity of community interaction that is moderated and transparent.  
 
● Legal obligations relating to data protection and user privacy.  
 
● Accessibility and usability expectations for diverse user groups.  
 
These drivers inform both what the system must do and how it must behave, shaping functional 
and non-functional requirements simultaneously. 
 
6.2 Comparison of Systems 
Existing systems within the pet care domain were examined during product research to elicit 
implicit and explicit requirements. Informational platforms demonstrate the importance of clear 
content categorisation but reveal weaknesses in engagement and navig ation efficiency. Adoption 
platforms highlight the value of searchable listings but often lack educational context that supports 
responsible decision-making. Veterinary directory systems emphasise efficient search functionality 
yet provide limited interpretive support for users evaluating services. 
18 
Community-based platforms illustrate the demand for peer interaction but expose the risks of 
unmoderated environments. From these comparisons, several requirements emerge indirectly, such 
as the need for guided navigation, contextual explanations, visible moderation, and integration 
between informational and interactive components. 
Rather than replicating existing systems, the proposed platform synthesises these insights to define 
requirements that address recurring usability and governance gaps. 
 
6.3 Requirements Elicitation 
Formal primary research methods such as large-scale questionnaires or interviews were considered 
but deemed impractical within the scope and timeline of the project. Instead, requirements 
elicitation was conducted through secondary research, reflective analysis, and informal observation 
of user behaviour within existing platforms and online communities. 
This approach, while limited in scale, is appropriate for an academic project focused on conceptual 
design and usability justification. Insights from literature and platform analysis were triangulated 
to identify consistent user needs and pain points. Ethical considerations were applied to ensure that 
assumptions about user behaviour remained cautious and justifiable. 
This elicitation process prioritised feasibility and relevance over breadth, ensuring that identified 
requirements could be realistically implemented and evaluated within the project constraints. 
 
6.4 Functional Requirements 
The functional requirements define the core capabilities of the system and provide the foundation 
for subsequent design and implementation. At a minimum, the system must support the following 
functions: 
● User registration and authentication to enable personalised interaction.  
 
● Access to structured educational content on cat and kitten care.  
 
● Browsing and searching adoption listings with clear contextual information.  
 
● Discovery of veterinary services using location -based search and user reviews.  
 
● Participation in community forums, including posting, replying, and reporting content.  
 
19 
● Administrative moderation tools for managing user -generated content.  
 
These functional requirements directly address the needs identified through literature and product 
research and ensure that the platform delivers meaningful value beyond static information 
provision. 
 
6.5 Non-Functional Requirements 
Non-functional requirements define how the system performs and how it aligns with earlier legal, 
social, ethical, and professional considerations. These requirements include: 
● Usability: The interface must be intuitive, with clear navigation structures and minimal 
cognitive load.  
 
● Accessibility: Compliance with recognised accessibility guidelines is required to ensure 
inclusivity. 
 
● Performance: Pages must load efficiently across devices and network conditions.  
 
● Security: User data must be protected through secure authentication and storage 
mechanisms. 
 
● Reliability: Core features must function consistently without data loss or unexpected 
behaviour. 
 
● Maintainability: The system should support content updates and moderation without 
extensive technical overhead.  
 
Compliance with these non-functional requirements reinforces the platform’s ethical responsibility 
and professional credibility. They ensure that legal obligations are met, social risks are mitigated, 
and user trust is supported through reliable and transparent system behaviour. 
 
 
 
7. Design

The design of the PurrfectStart platform is driven by the need to translate abstract requirements 
into concrete structures that support usability, accessibility, and ethical responsibility. Rather than 
treating design as a discrete phase following requirem ents, this project recognises the blurred 
boundary between requirements and design, particularly where user interaction is concerned. As 
noted by Šilingas and Butleris (2015), artefacts such as use -case representations often function 
simultaneously as requirement definitions and design instruments. 
The design approach begins with identifying core user interactions and translating these into high-
level interaction flows. These flows inform decisions regarding page structure, navigation 
hierarchy, and content grouping. While formal UML use-case notation can be used to express these 
interactions, the emphasis in this project is on their functional meaning rather than strict syntactic 
compliance. The use of interaction scenarios allows design decisions to remain grounded in user 
goals rather than abstract system components. 
Other UML artefacts, such as component or activity diagrams, are treated as supportive design 
tools rather than ends in themselves. Their role is to clarify system boundaries, data movement, and 
responsibilities between components, particularly where these overlap with testing considerations 
such as error handling and edge cases. This pragmatic use of modelling supports clarity without 
imposing unnecessary complexity. 
Overall, the design process is iterative and exploratory. Early design decisions are revisited as 
insights emerge from prototyping and evaluation. This reflects the reality of user -centred design, 
where understanding of the problem space evolves through engagement with design artefacts rather 
than being fully resolved at the outset. 
 
8. Prototype 
Prototyping plays a central role in the development of the PurrfectStart platform, serving as both a 
design exploration tool and a mechanism for evaluating usability assumptions. Rather than 
producing a single definitive prototype, the project adopts an in cremental approach in which 
multiple prototypes are developed, reviewed, and refined. 
The initial prototype focuses on structural representation, including page layout, navigation flow, 
and content hierarchy. This low -fidelity prototype enables early identification of usability issues 
without the overhead of full implementation. Subsequent prototypes introduce increasing levels of 
interactivity, allowing features such as search functionality, content browsing, and community 
interaction to be explored in a more realistic context. 
Each iteration of the prototype responds to emerging insights regarding user effort, clarity, and 
interaction friction. The progression from low -fidelity to higher -fidelity prototypes supports 
reflective evaluation and reduces the risk of committing premat urely to ineffective design 
decisions. This approach aligns with the project’s emphasis on learning through making rather than 
assuming correctness at the outset.

8.1 Review of Technology 
The review of technology is conducted in direct support of the prototyping process rather than as 
an abstract technical comparison. The technologies considered are evaluated according to their 
suitability for implementing the identified requirements within the project’s scope and constraints. 
For data storage and content management, the need for a flexible, maintainable system capable of 
supporting user-generated content and structured information was identified. Several database and 
content management options were considered, including bespoke relational database solutions and 
integrated content management systems. A full custom database solution was ultimately excluded 
due to the development overhead and limited added value within an academic project context. 
Similarly, multiple web development approaches were reviewed, ranging from fully custom 
frameworks to established website platforms with extensible plugin ecosystems. While custom 
frameworks offer greater control, they also introduce significant complexity  and risk within a 
constrained timeframe. Platform-based solutions provide integrated hosting, security features, and 
rapid prototyping capabilities, making them more appropriate for iterative design and usability 
evaluation. 
Three candidate platforms were examined in greater detail based on extensibility, usability support, 
accessibility features, and community ecosystem. The selected platform was chosen not because it 
was the most technically sophisticated, but because it off ered the best balance between 
functionality, reliability, and development efficiency. This decision reflects a pragmatic 
prioritisation of usability outcomes over technical novelty. 
 
9. Reflection

development. Initial assumptions regarding user needs, platform capabilities, and development 
structure were challenged as the project progressed, reinforcing the importance of remaining open

adherence to predefined plans. The iterative movement between research, design, and prototyping 
revealed mismatches between initial expectations and practical realities, particularly in relation to 
usability and content organisation. Acknowledging these mismatches allowed the project to evolve 
in a more grounded and defensible direction. 
From a personal development perspective, the project underscored the limitations of relying on 
formal methodologies as substitutes for critical thinking. While methodological frameworks 
provide useful guidance, meaningful progress depended on reflective ju dgement, ethical
